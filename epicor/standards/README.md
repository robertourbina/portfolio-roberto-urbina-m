# Epicor Portfolio Documentation Standards

Welcome to the **Epicor Portfolio Documentation Standards**.

This folder defines the documentation, organization, and presentation standards used throughout the Epicor ERP section of this portfolio.

The objective of these standards is to ensure that every example follows a consistent structure, making the portfolio easier to navigate, maintain, and understand.

Rather than presenting isolated technical examples, this portfolio is designed as a professional knowledge base that demonstrates business analysis, solution design, ERP implementation, testing, and technical documentation.

---

# Portfolio Philosophy

The purpose of this portfolio is not only to demonstrate technical knowledge of Epicor ERP, but also to present complete business solutions in a structured and professional manner.

Each example is documented as a **case study**, following the same methodology used during ERP implementation projects.

Every case study explains:

* The business problem.
* The business requirements.
* The proposed solution.
* The Epicor implementation.
* The validation process.
* The business value delivered.

This approach reflects the responsibilities of an Epicor ERP Application Specialist or Functional Consultant.

---

# Documentation Principles

The following principles apply to every Epicor example contained in this repository.

## Consistency

Every case study follows the same document structure, section order, naming conventions, and visual style.

---

## Business First

Every example begins by explaining the business challenge before introducing the technical implementation.

The objective is to demonstrate how Epicor ERP solves real business problems.

---

## Version Independence

Examples are designed around Epicor ERP concepts rather than a specific software release.

Whenever possible, documentation remains compatible with both Epicor ERP 10.x concepts and Epicor Kinetic concepts.

---

## Clear Documentation

Each section should be concise, well organized, and easy to understand by both technical and functional audiences.

---

## Practical Examples

Every solution represents a realistic ERP business scenario that could be implemented within an organization.
